Connect Four

Connect Four is a two-player connection board game, in which the players choose a color and then take turns dropping colored discs into a seven-column, six-row vertically suspended grid. The objective of the game is to be the first to form a horizontal, vertical, or diagonal line of four of one's own discs.

Rules:

  • The game is played on a grid that has 7 columns and 6 rows. Each cell in the grid can hold one disc.
  • Each player chooses a color for their discs (usually red or yellow). The game starts with an empty grid.
  • Players take turns dropping one of their colored discs into any of the seven columns. The disc falls down to the lowest available cell in the chosen column.
  • A player wins the game if they have four of their colored discs connected in a row, either horizontally, vertically, or diagonally.
  • If the entire grid is filled with discs and no player has a winning connection of four discs, the game ends in a draw.
  • Once a game is concluded, the players can start a new game, usually with the loser of the previous game going first.
